Eric Heritage

Health Physics, MASc

My research involves obtaining more accurate contact dosimetry estimates for sealed gamma sources, that better match observed physical symptoms.  I will be designing an experiment where the surface electrons and gamma components of the contact dose rate, for a sealed gamma source, will be measured separately.  A large magnet will be used to bend the surface electrons away from the gamma dose measurement. 

contact: eric.heritage@uoit.ca


Martin Magill

Computational Modelling, MSc

My thesis work is aimed at modelling the DNA damage caused by ionizing radiation. I want to establish a comprehensive model that starts with quantum-mechanical electron transport through the cell, computes free radical formation, and then models the resulting molecular dynamics. I also have an assortment of other research interests. My poisons of choice are absinthe and Trappist beer. And I enjoy going mushroom hunting with my faithful truffle dog Nyx.

contact: martin.magill@uoit.ca

Arif Qureshi

Nuclear Engineering

MASc: Spent Fuel Transportation

Arif Qureshi is currently completing his Master of Engineering studies at the University Of Ontario Institute Of Technology (UOIT) in a partnership with the Nuclear Waste Management Organization (NWMO). In 2012 he completed an internship with the NWMO, resulting in a joint project for his masters studies. He is currently completing work related to spent fuel transportation packages.  Balancing academics and leadership are very important to him; having served as President of his school’s Engineering Society and sitting on the Board of Directors of the Student Association for two consecutive years. Additionally, he has published several papers and won the ASME Best Student Paper, North America & Europe and the Best Student Paper at the International Conference on Nuclear Engineering (ICONE) in Osaka, Japan.  His future goals are to work on projects related to spent fuel transportation and the establishment of a long term repository for Canada’s used fuel.

contact: arif.@uoit.ca
