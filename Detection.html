Radiation Detection


Radiation Triage Mask
C. Brown

The radiation triage mask (RTM) is a device used to detect radiation in the breath and on the face of a person who may have inhaled particles following an accidental or intentional release. The mask makes use of a Geiger-Muller beta/gamma detector and a Cadmium-Zinc-Telluride (CZT) gamma spectrometer to quantify and identify radionuclides. The project is focused on simple and reliable operation. This prototype represents the third generation of hardware and software development, which was started in 2007. Availability of 3D printing has enabled the size to be reduced dramatically. The primary Emergency Response mode quickly triages patients. If radiation levels above background are detected after a short measurement, a longer measurement can then be performed to identify the radionuclide.
